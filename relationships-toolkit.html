Relationships & Social Skills

A complete coaching toolkit — session plans, a role-play bank, and a self-assessment with personalised insights — for building real interpersonal skill in teenagers.

The Module

Fourteen units, one through-line: relate well, on purpose.

The arc moves from the relationship a teen has with themselves outward — to friends, family, peers, and the people they'll fall out with and lose. Each unit is a self-contained 45–60 minute one-to-one session. Run them in order, or pull the one a student needs this week.

How to use this toolkit

1
Open with the self-assessment. Have the student complete it in session one to surface a personal starting profile and pick priority units.
2
Run a session plan. Each follows the same rhythm — check-in, teach, practise, reflect, mission — so students learn the shape and settle quickly.
3
Pull a scenario. The role-play bank is filtered by theme — drop one into any session for live practice instead of talk.
4
Re-assess after 6–8 weeks. Re-run the self-assessment to make growth visible to the student and the parent.

Self-Assessment

Where do your people-skills stand today?

Thirty honest questions across ten areas. There are no right answers and no scores to pass — just a snapshot to grow from. Answer for how things actually are, not how you wish they were.

Before you begin

Read each statement and pick how true it feels for you right now. The whole thing takes about six minutes. Your answers stay on this screen.
